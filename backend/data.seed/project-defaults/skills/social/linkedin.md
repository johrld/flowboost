# LinkedIn Post Skill

## Tone & Voice

- Professional but approachable
- Thought-leadership positioning
- First person or brand voice (not generic)
- No corporate jargon without substance

## Structure

1. **Hook** (1-2 sentences) — grab attention in the feed
2. **Context** (2-3 sentences) — why this matters
3. **Value** (3-5 bullet points or short paragraphs) — the insight
4. **CTA** (1 sentence) — question or call to action
5. **Hashtags** (separate line at the end)

## Hook Patterns

- Contrarian: "Most people think X. They're wrong."
- Data: "We analyzed 500 posts and found..."
- Story: "Last week, something changed..."
- Direct value: "3 things I wish I knew about..."
- Question: "What if everything you knew about X was outdated?"

## Formatting

- Short paragraphs (1-2 sentences max)
- Line breaks between every thought for mobile readability
- Use bullet points sparingly (max 1 list per post)
- No emojis in the first line
- Optimal length: 800-1500 characters

## Do

- Share actionable insights, not platitudes
- Ask genuine questions that invite discussion
- Reference specific experience or data
- Include one clear CTA

## Don't

- Start with "Excited to announce..."
- Use clickbait hooks that don't deliver
- Write walls of unbroken text
- Use more than 5 hashtags
- Be preachy or self-congratulatory
