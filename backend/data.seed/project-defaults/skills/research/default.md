# Research Skill

## What to Research

1. **Primary Keyword** — the main search term for this topic
   - Check search volume (prefer 500+ monthly)
   - Check difficulty (prefer under 50)
   - Match user intent

2. **Secondary Keywords** (3-5) — related terms
   - Synonyms and variations
   - Related subtopics
   - "People also ask" queries

3. **Long-tail Keywords** (2-3) — specific phrases
   - Question-format queries
   - "How to..." and "Best..." variations
   - Low competition, high intent

4. **Competitor Analysis**
   - Top 3 search results for primary keyword
   - What they cover well
   - What gaps exist (topics they miss)
   - Their word count and structure

5. **Search Intent**
   - Informational: user wants to learn
   - How-to: user wants step-by-step instructions
   - Transactional: user wants to buy/download
   - Navigational: user looking for a specific page

## How to Research

1. Use WebSearch for keyword research queries
2. Use WebFetch to analyze top competitor pages
3. Read the project's content index to find existing content (avoid overlap)
4. Check the project's brand voice for tone alignment
5. Consider the briefing inputs (user's notes, voice memos, URLs) as source material

## Output Quality

- Be specific with numbers (search volume, difficulty scores)
- Quote competitor insights with specifics ("headspace.com ranks #3 with 2,400 words")
- Suggest an angle that differentiates from competitors
- Recommend a realistic word count based on competition
